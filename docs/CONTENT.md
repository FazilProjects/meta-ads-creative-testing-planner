# Content Guide

## Voice

The content should sound professional, clear, and useful. It should reflect a learning-focused portfolio project by Fazil Waseem.

Use:

- Practical marketing language
- Honest expectations
- Clear labels
- Direct action prompts
- Simple strategic explanations

Avoid:

- Guaranteed performance claims
- Claims of official Meta affiliation
- Overly technical jargon
- Hype-heavy language
- Claims that the tool creates or manages real ads

## Required Disclaimer

The project should make clear that it is a local creative planning dashboard and does not connect to Meta Ads or external ad platforms.

Suggested wording:

> This is a local creative planning dashboard. It is not connected to Meta Ads or any external platform.

## Planning Inputs

The local planning logic should be based on:

- Campaign objective
- Target audience
- Audience segment
- Product or service
- Offer
- Pain point
- Promise or outcome

## Planning Outputs

The dashboard should generate or display:

- Audience segment planner
- Creative angles
- Hook ideas
- Primary text variations
- Headline variations
- CTA suggestions
- Creative testing matrix
- Budget testing plan
- Winning ad checklist
- Exportable plan

## Creative Angle Types

Recommended angle families:

- Pain-aware
- Outcome-led
- Offer-focused
- Trust proof
- Objection handling
- Comparison
- Urgency or timing
- Education-first

## Winning Ad Criteria

A winning ad should be judged by more than one metric. Criteria can include:

- Strong hook and thumb-stop signal
- Relevant audience response
- Improved primary KPI
- Credible lead or engagement quality
- Clear message-to-offer fit
- Repeatable concept that can become more variants

## Honest Claims Guidance

Use phrases like:

- "Plan"
- "Suggest"
- "Generate ideas"
- "Testing direction"
- "Creative planning"
- "Decision rule"

Avoid phrases like:

- "Guaranteed winner"
- "Official Meta tool"
- "Launch campaigns"
- "Connect ad account"
- "Automatically optimizes ads"
- "Assured ROAS"
